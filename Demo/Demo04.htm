Now lets have a closer look at the WinBlit Window in the frame below.

The Window is divided into 4 areas , from top to bottom these are the Toolbar , the Text Window, the Trackbar and the status bar at the bottom.

ToolBar - Each icon on the toolbar represents a unique function (you have already tried the "play" function). The toolbar is the first of 3 possible ways you can operate WinBlit.

Text Window  -  This is of course where the text is flashed to you in the manner you have chosen. As you will see later you have an enormous amount of control over how text appears and how it is flashed to you. .

Trackbar - Beneath the text window you can see the trackbar which moves left to right as your reading progresses through a document. You can change your place in a document by clicking on the trackbar cursor and dragging it to the left or to the right.

Statusbar - At the bottom of the Window you can see the status bar. When you are reading with WinBlit this displays some basic statistics on your reading speed.
